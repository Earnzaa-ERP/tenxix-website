Honest answers

Questions women actually ask us.

I've had three children. Is it too late for me?
No. The pelvic floor is a muscle, and muscles respond to training at any age. Women who started in their fifties and sixties report the same strength recovery as younger women — it just takes a few more weeks of consistent use. Six children, sixty years old — your body still wants to come back.
Does it really tighten, or is that just marketing?
The honest answer: it strengthens the pelvic floor muscle, which is what creates the "tightness" women describe. When that muscle is stronger, sensation increases for you and grip increases for him. Same principle pelvic physiotherapists use — at home, with resistance you can feel. Most women report noticeable difference in 4–6 weeks.
How long until I see a difference in my arms?
Two to three weeks for firmness you can feel. Six weeks for a difference others notice. Twelve weeks for sleeveless confidence. The trick is consistency — five minutes daily beats forty minutes once a week, every time.
I leak when I sneeze. Will this really help?
Yes, very likely. Stress urinary incontinence — the kind that happens when you sneeze, laugh or lift — is almost always caused by a weak pelvic floor. Strengthening that muscle with consistent resistance training is the first-line treatment recommended by doctors worldwide. Most women see significant improvement in 6–8 weeks.
How soon after giving birth can I use it?
Wait until your six-week postnatal check-up and get clearance from your doctor — especially after a c-section or vaginal tearing. After that, start gently: lowest resistance, fewer reps, build up weekly. Your body has just done the hardest physical work of its life. Be patient with it.
How is this different from doing free Kegel exercises?
Most women do Kegels wrong. Without resistance you can't feel whether the right muscle is firing. With the Reset, the squeeze gives you immediate feedback — you know the muscle is engaged, and you can progressively load it. It's the difference between air-squats forever and using actual weight. Same exercise. Real results.
Is it discreet? My husband / children / staff will see it.
Yes. It looks like a small piece of fitness equipment for inner-thigh toning — because that's one of the things it does. Most women keep it next to the bed or on a closet shelf. No one will know what you're actually using it for unless you tell them.
